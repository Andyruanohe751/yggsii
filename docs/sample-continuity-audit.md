# Sample Continuity Audit

Project: The Cartography of Roots

This is a sample client-facing audit output for the seeded Yggsii demo project.

The point is not that this tiny story is broken. The point is to demonstrate the shape of a paid continuity pass and the kinds of issues or strengths Yggsii can surface quickly.

## Executive summary

The current story spine is coherent and readable.

Chronology is clean across four scenes and two days. Cast handoff is strong, with Mira acting as the clear continuity anchor through every scene.

The main structural watchpoint is not contradiction. It is relationship compression. Seline appears once, with strong story energy, then disappears immediately. Brother Cael appears only at the descent stage, which makes the Root Archive thread feel introduced late unless that delay is intentional.

If this were a real client manuscript, I would not call for a rewrite. I would call for one follow-up pass on continuity of pressure, especially around who carries forward after the Lantern Market turn.

## What is working

### 1. Chronology is legible

The time labels progress cleanly from Day 1, Dawn to Day 2, Morning.

There are no obvious order inversions or duplicate-time ambiguities in the current slice.

### 2. Character continuity has a clear anchor

Mira appears in all four scenes.

That gives the story a reliable continuity spine and lowers the risk of early-reader disorientation.

### 3. Location continuity is sensible

Lantern Market holds the middle beat across two consecutive scenes.

That makes the transition from bargain to interception feel spatially grounded rather than arbitrary.

### 4. Ensemble growth is controlled

The cast expands from solo scene, to pair scene, to three-character pressure scene, then to a different three-character descent scene.

That progression feels deliberate rather than chaotic.

## Likely continuity watchpoints

### 1. Seline is introduced strongly, then drops out immediately

Risk:
A high-energy rival entry followed by instant absence can make the story feel like it teased a larger collision than it delivered.

What to check:
- Is Seline still exerting pressure in Scene 4, even if off-page.
- Does Mira or Tarin carry forward any consequence from Seline's offer.
- Is there a later callback soon enough to prevent the rival thread from cooling.

### 2. Brother Cael enters only at the archive threshold

Risk:
Cael may read as a plot function rather than a living part of the world if he arrives only when the archive becomes necessary.

What to check:
- Whether Cael should be foreshadowed earlier by name, rumour, doctrine, or institutional presence.
- Whether the Root Archive is mentioned before the descent scene.

### 3. Tarin's continuity role is strong, but his leverage arc needs tracking

Risk:
Tarin is present from Scene 2 onward, which is good, but his motive may flatten into generic companion energy if the bargain terms stop mattering.

What to check:
- What secret Mira offered in Scene 2.
- Whether Tarin's price, debt, or risk changes after Seline enters the picture.
- Whether Scene 4 reflects any evolution in trust.

## Relationship and co-presence notes

- Mira and Tarin are currently the strongest active pair because they persist across three consecutive scenes.
- Mira and Seline currently read as a promising but underdeveloped rivalry signal.
- Tarin and Seline share the same single pressure scene, which may be enough for tension setup but not yet enough for a durable triangle.
- Mira, Tarin, and Brother Cael form the descent ensemble, but the handoff from rival pressure to keeper guidance should be checked so the emotional baton does not get dropped.

## Priority fixes if this were a real client project

### High priority

Ensure Scene 4 carries at least one explicit aftereffect from Scene 3.

That could be suspicion, urgency, altered route choice, or a strategic decision shaped by Seline's intervention.

### Medium priority

Seed Brother Cael or the Root Archive earlier.

Even one short reference can make his arrival feel inevitable rather than conveniently timed.

### Medium priority

Track Tarin's bargain terms.

If the initial deal matters, it should echo beyond the agreement scene.

## Audit conclusion

This sample project does not show continuity failure. It shows continuity potential.

The current value of the audit is in identifying pressure-drop risk, under-foreshadowed elements, and relationships that are structurally promising but not yet fully paid off.

That is exactly the kind of pass Yggsii is suited to support.
