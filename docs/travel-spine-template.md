# Travel Spine Template

Use this when a story starts losing causality once people begin moving.

This is especially useful for:
- campaigns being adapted into prose
- road stories
- quest structures
- military or chase narratives
- any project where urgency, distance, and consequence can drift apart

## Purpose

A travel spine keeps movement legible.

It helps the audit track:
- where the cast starts
- where they go next
- how much time passes
- what pressure follows them
- what changes because of the move

Without this, travel sections often become vague connective tissue and quietly damage continuity.

## How to use it

Create one row for each meaningful movement step.

A "movement step" can be:
- departure from a base location
- arrival at a temporary stop
- a skipped travel segment that still matters for timing
- retreat, detour, or forced redirection

Use the spine before prose adaptation hardens fuzzy recap logic into fake certainty.

## Minimal version, if the material is rough

If the source is messy, track only:
- where they started
- where they ended up
- how long it seems to have taken
- what threat or urgency should still be active
- what changed because of the move

## Common failure modes this catches

### 1. Travel time does not match urgency

The story says the danger is immediate, but the cast apparently spends days moving with no cost.

### 2. Locations feel disconnected

The cast arrives somewhere new, but nothing from the previous place seems to carry forward.

### 3. Pursuing pressure vanishes in transit

A rival, storm, warband, debt, or deadline matters intensely in one scene and evaporates during movement.

### 4. The group composition drifts without explanation

Someone is suddenly present or absent after a travel segment and the draft never accounts for it.

## Audit prompts to pair with the spine

Ask these while filling it out:
- Why did they leave now, not earlier or later?
- What still matters during the move?
- What should have changed by the time they arrive?
- Does the elapsed time fit the claimed urgency?
- If a pursuing force exists, what evidence of it reaches the next stop?

## Why this matters commercially

Travel blur is one of those problems writers often feel but cannot name.

A travel spine makes the issue concrete.

That helps the continuity-audit offer feel practical rather than mystical, especially for campaign adaptation, adventure fiction, and ensemble stories under pressure.
