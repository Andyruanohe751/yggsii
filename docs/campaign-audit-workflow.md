# Campaign Continuity Audit Workflow

This note bundles the campaign-specific audit tools into one working sequence.

The point is to make the continuity-audit offer feel like a method, not just a loose pile of documents.

Use this for campaign adaptation, messy ensemble fiction, or any project where recap logic, movement, secrets, and cast drift are all trying to betray each other at once.

## When to use this workflow

Reach for this sequence when the project has at least two of the following:
- ordered session recaps rather than polished scenes
- hidden knowledge or staggered reveals
- travel arcs or movement-heavy structure
- character absences, rotating focus, or weak reintegration
- pressure from factions, rivals, debts, or deadlines that should persist across multiple stops

## Workflow sequence

### 1. Reconstruct the basic spine

Start with ordered sessions, scenes, or recap blocks.

Goal:
Confirm that the story has a usable sequence before you diagnose higher-order problems.

Questions:
- What is the current order of events?
- Where are the obvious gaps, skips, or recap compressions?
- Is the material coherent enough for a full audit, or should it be scoped down to one arc?

If the answer is "barely coherent," do a pilot audit first.

## 2. Build the reveal ledger

Use `reveal-ledger-template.md`.

Goal:
Separate world truth, character belief, and reader knowledge before hindsight language poisons the draft.

Focus on:
- hidden agendas
- disputed facts
- secrets and reveals
- canon constraints
- unreliable viewpoints

Questions:
- Who knows what, when?
- What is only suspected?
- What should the reader know at each stage?
- Where is the draft explaining too much too early?

## 3. Build the travel spine

Use `travel-spine-template.md`.

Goal:
Make movement causal rather than decorative.

Focus on:
- departures and arrivals
- elapsed time
- moving pressure
- consequences that should still be active on arrival

Questions:
- Why did they leave now?
- Does the travel time fit the urgency claims?
- What pressure follows them into the next location?
- What changes because of the move?

## 4. Build the absence tracker

Use `absence-tracking-template.md`.

Goal:
Turn character dropout from table logistics into legible story structure.

Focus on:
- absences across multiple sessions or scenes
- weak-presence drift
- in-world acknowledgement
- altered group dynamics
- return payoff

Questions:
- Does the story notice the absence?
- Who reacts to it, and how?
- What becomes harder, riskier, or emotionally different?
- Does the return feel intentional, or merely administrative?

## 5. Cross-check persistent pressure

Now compare the three tools against each other.

Goal:
See whether the same story pressure survives across knowledge, movement, and cast continuity.

Typical checks:
- Does a faction threat stay alive across the travel spine, or vanish between stops?
- Does a missing character affect what others know or suspect?
- Does a reveal still land correctly after movement and absence gaps are accounted for?
- Are there scenes where all three tools imply the draft is pretending continuity that has not been earned?

## 6. Write the audit memo

Only after the three tool passes should the client-facing memo be written.

Goal:
Translate structural findings into a clean set of risks, strengths, and highest-value fixes.

Suggested memo shape:
- executive summary
- what is working
- highest-risk continuity threads
- specific fixes in priority order
- optional note on what can wait

Do not dump raw tooling at the client unless that is part of the offer. The tools are diagnostic scaffolding, not necessarily the product itself.

## Fast triage version

If time or material quality is poor, use this compressed sequence:
- reconstruct order
- build only the reveal ledger for knowledge-state risk
- build either the travel spine or absence tracker, whichever is clearly more threatening
- write a narrower memo with explicit scope limits

## What this workflow catches well

- secrets revealed too early or too late
- movement that breaks urgency or causality
- cast absences that read as forgetfulness
- recap summaries that flatten uncertainty into false certainty
- projects where the real continuity risk is distributed across multiple systems, not one obvious contradiction

## What this workflow does not replace

- line editing
- prose polish
- full developmental editing
- detailed worldbuilding design
- final publication QA

It is a continuity and structural legibility pass.
